About Me

    My linguistic research focuses on semantics and pragmatics, with a special interest in quantifiers, presupposition, attitude predicates, and the projection phenomena. I use logic and experimental methods to model and understand natural languages. My current work found the negative quantifier “none” have extremely interesting properties, which leads to my further investigation into the negation phenomenon. I believe my research will provide insights into key properties of the human linguistic system.
    Moreover, I conduct research on the semantics of emoji, specializing in the relation between emoji and their accompanying text.
    In addition to linguistics, I am also conducting research on Mongolian studies, including Mongolian language and translation, history, and international relationships.
