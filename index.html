About Me

    My linguistic research focuses on semantics and pragmatics, with a special interest in quantifiers, presupposition, attitude predicates, and the projection phenomena. I use logic and experimental methods to model and understand natural languages. My first work focuses on presupposition projections out of quantifiers, and it found the negative quantifier “none” has extremely interesting properties, which led to my further investigation into the negation phenomenon. My second work concerns presuppositions of additives under attitude verbs, with an analysis under the bidimensional semantics framework. Currently, I'm working on projection properties of repetitives. I believe my research will provide insights into key properties of the human linguistic system.
    Moreover, I research the semantics of emoji, specializing in the relation between emoji and their accompanying text.
    In addition to linguistics, I am also conducting research on Mongolian studies, including Mongolian language and translation, history, and international relationships.
